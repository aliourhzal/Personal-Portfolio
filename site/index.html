james webb space telescope

Here's a time line of james webb's career

  • 1989: The Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI) and NASA co-host the Next Generation Space Telescope Workshop at STScI.
  • 1995-1996: An STScI committee recommends a significantly larger telescope capable of observing infrared light.
  • 1997: NASA selects teams from the Goddard Space Flight Center, TRW, and Ball Aerospace to fine-tune the telescope's technical and financial requirements.
  • 1999: Lockheed Martin, Ball Aerospace, and TRW (also partnering with Kodak and ATK) conduct Phase A mission studies
  • 2002: Based on two Phase A studies, NASA selects the design of TRW/Ball Aerospace to continue in Phase B detailed design studies, which examine the performance and cost of the chosen design.
  • 2004: Construction begins on certain telescope parts that require extensive, long-term work-in particular, Webb's science instruments and the 18 segments of the primary mirro
  • 2005: NASA approves the use of the European Space Agency's Ariane 5 rocket to launch Webb into space..
  • 2009: The Integrated Science Instrument Module (ISIM) structure, built to house Webb's four science instruments, arrives at Goddard Space Flight Center for testing.
  • 2014: Manufacturing of the spacecraft parts (such as fuel tanks, gyroscopes, and solar panels) begins.
  • 2017: The Optical Telescope Element successfully undergoes cryogenic testing in a giant thermal vacuum chamber called Chamber A at Johnson Space Center.
  • 2020: Webb is fully folded for the first time and completes final environmental testing to prove it can withstand the shaking and jostling of the launch environment.
  • 2021: ASA's James Webb Space Telescope is launched December 25 at 7:20 a.m. EST on an Ariane 5 rocket from Europe's Spaceport in French Guiana.
